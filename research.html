Copyright, Creative Common (CC) Licenses, Fair Use:

Copyright principles:

Copyright automatically applies when something is published. In NZ, creators do not have to register it like a trademark or patent.
The © symbol is only a reminder. Copyright still applies even if the © is not displayed.
Copyright protects everything that is published which includes: ✶Print material ✶Computer programs ✶Dramatic performances ✶Music ✶Art ✶Page layouts ✶Photos
Copyright lasts more than a lifetime. Copyright in NZ on literary, dramatic, artistic and musical works stays in place for the creators lifetime and up to 50 years after their death.
Copyright Reference

Creative Commons (CC) licences:

Creative Commons licences were created for copyright owners that wanted to share their work without receiving payment and/or royalties. Creative Commons licences use icons and acronyms to show how the owner of the work wants to be acknowledged and the limitations of how the work is allowed to be used.
CC Reference

Fair Use:

Fair use does not apply in New Zealand. It is an American term with an American application. Often used in legal defence by people accused of breaching someone else's copyright. New Zealand uses “Fair Dealing” which is defined as a set of privileges that someone can legally exercise, without the permission of the copyright owner. Fair dealing allows someone to use copyrighted material, without permission for the purpose of research, private study, criticism, review and news reporting.
Fair Use Reference

Privacy & Privacy Policy:

Privacy Policy:

A privacy policy/statement tells people how their information will be collected, used and disclosed. The NZ Privacy Act 2020 requires New Zealand organisations to be transparent about when, why and how they collect personal information.

Search Engine Optimisation (SEO):

Improving The Visibility of a Webpage:

Optimising HTML:
Constantly checking that your website's code is working well and nothing in the code is making it lag.

Sharing your website on social media:
Link sharing drives more traffic to your website.

User-Friendly Interface:
Make it accessible and easy to understand. Links, buttons etc should all be working. No one wants to use a website that frustrates them.

Keyword Targeting:
Commonly searched for phrases or keywords are crucial in search engine optimisation. The more keywords you use the more people will use your website.

Humans First Approach:
Write your website for humans first and search engines second, so it can be more easily understood.

Remove Anything Unnecessary:
Anything that slows down the website has got to go. That includes useless comments and code that doesn't do anything. Only have code that is useful.

Three major factors in choosing a web host:



Must be safe and have good security. No one wants their information being hacked and stolen.

Must be fast and reliable. No one will use the website if it takes too long to load or doesn't load at all.

5 Most Common Cyber Attacks & How To Prevent Them:

Password based attack:

Hackers do many things to get through someone's password like guessing and using password cracking software. The best way to prevent this is to create long passwords and unique passwords. The same password should also not be used twice.

Denial of service attacks:

Hackers and scammers send an overload of data to a website making it unusable. The best way to prevent this from happening is to constantly monitor traffic. Traffic monitoring tools can be used.

Social engineering attacks:

Phishing is how many people are tricked into clicking a suspicious link. The best way to prevent this is to educate employees, be cautious, use phishing detection tools and use two factor authentication.

Man-in-the-middle Attacks:

A hacker inserts themself into a situation to eavesdrop or pretends to be someone involved to steal sensitive information. To prevent this it's important to pay attention to security certificate warnings, use HTTPs enabled websites and use secured networks.

Malware attacks

Malicious software: viruses, worms, ransomware, spyware etc Can cause the loss of important and sensitive information and money too. To prevent this from happening you should browse cautiously and not download untrusted files or email attachments.
